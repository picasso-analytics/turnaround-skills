# Track 2 — Time-Phased Readiness

The physical / procurement / logistics track. **Lighter and broader** than the Execution
Control Systems track — the deep 7-step interrogation is reserved for the crown jewels. This
track's job is a **T-minus-calibrated sweep** that catches **showstoppers and off-track items**:
"scope isn't frozen and you're six weeks out," "you haven't secured the pipefitters,"
"long-leads still aren't received."

It still uses the paper-vs-real lens and the three readiness tells (show the tool · show last TA
· walk the scenario) — just a broader, shallower pass, not a battle-test of each item.

## How T-minus calibration works

Every domain is judged against **where the client sits in the runway**. The same status means
different things at different points:

- **Far out (e.g., T-6mo):** the bar is *plans and commitments* — scope defined, long-leads on
  order with expedite dates, contractor selected. "On order" is fine.
- **Mid (e.g., T-3mo):** the bar is *convergence* — scope frozen, materials arriving and being
  inspected, craft confirmed, procedures drafted.
- **Near (e.g., T-2wk):** the bar is *done and staged* — materials on-site/kitted, craft
  mobilized/badged, procedures approved. "On order" is now a **red flag**.

Rate each domain by **is this on-track for the runway position?** — not against a single fixed
"ready now" bar. A gap far out is amber-to-green if there's a credible path; the same gap near
execution is red because there's no time to close it. (This time-to-close weighting flows into
the verdict — see `rating-and-verdict.md`.)

## The 12 domains

For each: what it covers, and what an **off-track / showstopper** signal looks like.

1. **Scope** — defined, risk-ranked, and **frozen** by the right T-minus, with change control
   after freeze. *Off-track:* freeze date slid or never set; heavy line-item additions after
   "freeze"; scope not challenged (jobs carried because "we did it last time").

2. **Planning artifacts** — detailed job plans built; the schedule **resource-loaded**. *(The
   existence and loading of the schedule live here; its per-shift* running *is System 6.)*
   *Off-track:* header-level activities only; schedule not resource-loaded (which also
   disables Systems 1 and 4); open-ended logic, no defensible critical path.

3. **Materials procurement** — long-leads **ordered → received → inspected → kitted**,
   progressing by T-minus. **QA-on-returns** (inbound inspection/testing of shop-returned
   items) lives here. *(The during-execution* protection *control is System 2.)* *Off-track:*
   "ordered" near execution; no receiving inspection; returns not tested before install.

4. **Contractor mobilization** — contracts awarded; **skilled craft availability confirmed**
   (not just headcount promised); badging, orientation, lodging/per-diem, tools & equipment.
   *Off-track:* craft "committed" but unconfirmed in a tight regional labor market; mobilization
   logistics (badging/lodging) unplanned.

5. **Safety & permitting prep** — permit-to-work system set up; **isolation/LOTO and blind
   lists built and reconciled** to the P&IDs; JSAs; contractor safety qualification; safety
   staffing ratio; emergency response. *(The permit* throughput *control is System 1.)*
   *Off-track:* thick safety binder but blind lists not reconciled; permit system not staffed.

6. **Equipment / mechanical prep** — decontamination & cleaning plans; **scaffolding plan**;
   **crane/heavy-lift studies**; isolation/de-energization sequence. *Off-track:* heavy lifts
   with no lift study; scaffolding demand unplanned.

7. **Operations readiness** — **shutdown and startup sequences/procedures**; unit prep;
   utilities, N2; feedstock/product movement and **tankage** plans. *Off-track:* startup
   sequence not written; tankage/product movements unplanned.

8. **Logistics & site infrastructure** — laydown, trailers, parking, power, waste management,
   temporary facilities. *Off-track:* no laydown/waste plan for a large workforce.

9. **Organization & staffing** — TA org chart with **single-point accountability** and RACI;
   shift org filled with **named, competent** people. *(The meeting* rhythm *is System 7.)*
   *Off-track:* key roles TBD or held by someone learning on the job.

10. **Cost / budget** — AFE approved; estimate basis; contingency established. *Off-track:*
    unapproved budget; no estimate basis; no contingency.

11. **Readiness governance** — **T-minus stage-gate / milestone reviews actually held**, with
    action items **closed** (not just scheduled); independent/cold-eyes review. *Off-track:*
    milestone reviews skipped or held with open action items carried forward indefinitely.

12. **Startup / turnover** — punch-list & **systems-turnover** process; **PSSR** (pre-startup
    safety review) plan; commissioning approach. *Off-track:* no turnover/punch-list process;
    no PSSR plan. *(Often lighter-weight this far from startup — weight it to the runway.)*
